TSLA Trend Pullback

Instrument: Example option contract

Style: Intraday planning only

Core pattern: Uptrend + controlled pullback to VWAP / prior breakout level

Preconditions:

  • TSLA in clear intraday uptrend (higher highs & higher lows on 5m).
  • Price trading above VWAP and key prior support from the session.
  • Broader market (SPY/QQQ) not in active breakdown.

Entry rules:

  • Wait for a clear pullback into the marked support zone.
  • Enter only on a clean bounce candle that holds higher lows.
  • Use the contract profile defined for this strategy.

Exit / management:

  • First target is retest of prior intraday high; scale out into that push.
  • Cut quickly if price loses VWAP + prior swing low (thesis broken).
  • Avoid overstaying through sudden news or macro events.

Grade mapping: A if trend + level + bounce are all clean; B if one element is messy; C or lower if structure is unclear or market context disagrees.

Desk Overview

EdgeDesk is an example decision workspace. The UI is split into:

  • Advisor: planning support and structured context.
  • Session Review: outcomes, notes, and summary state.
  • Playbook: strategies, patterns, and reusable notes.
  • Controls: operating modes and visible guardrails.
  • Timeline: key decisions and review events.

This public demo is offline. It does not use live market data, connect to a broker, or execute trades.

Why the Playbook Matters

The Playbook is the first slice of your knowledge base. It captures:

  • Strategies: named playbook entries tied to real trades.
  • Patterns: price structures the strategies rely on.
  • Grade rules: what makes a setup A, B, or C for you.

Controls should always respect the playbook, the current risk profile, and the user's intent.

Controls and Guardrails

This panel shows how the workspace can keep guardrails visible:

  • Modes: Off, Observe, Assist, and Active.
  • Time rules: avoid rushed actions near sensitive parts of the session.
  • Pause: quickly stop activity and move every control into Observe mode.

The goal is simple: make the current operating posture clear at a glance.

What Appears Here

The timeline keeps important review events easy to scan:

  • New planning preview created.
  • Idea approved, paused, or sent back for review.
  • Actions, pauses, review notes, and guardrail changes.
  • Risk updates, mode changes, and pause events.

This makes it easier to understand what changed, when it changed, and why.
